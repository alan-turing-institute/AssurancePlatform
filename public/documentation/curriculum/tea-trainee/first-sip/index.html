Welcome & First Impressions (5 minutes)‚Äã

Imagine you're about to use a new AI system that screens job applications at your company. The vendor claims it's "fair and unbiased" ‚Äì but how can you be sure? How do you know it won't discriminate against certain groups? What evidence would convince you?

Today, you'll explore a powerful approach to answering these questions: the Trustworthy and Ethical Assurance (TEA) Platform. But rather than starting with definitions and theory, let's dive straight into a real example.

Your Journey In this module, you'll:
  1. Explore an actual assurance case for a Fair Recruitment AI system
  2. Discover how structured arguments build trust
  3. Learn the vocabulary naturally through interaction
  4. Reflect on what makes arguments convincing

The Core Elements‚Äã

You encountered five types of elements in the Fair Recruitment AI case:

  1. Goal (üéØ Green) - The main claim: "The AI recruitment system makes fair and unbiased hiring recommendations"
  2. Strategies (üé® Purple) - Three approaches to argue fairness: bias detection, transparency, and monitoring
  3. Property Claims (üìÑ Orange) - Specific, measurable statements like "Training data has been audited"
  4. Evidence (‚úÖ Cyan) - Concrete proof like audit reports and statistical analyses
  5. Context (‚ö†Ô∏è Grey) - Boundaries and definitions, like what "fairness" means legally

The Key Insight‚Äã

An assurance case works by decomposing a big claim (like "our AI is fair") into smaller, more manageable claims, and then providing evidence for each small claim. It's like building a pyramid of trust, where each level supports the one above it.

Next Steps‚Äã

Well done! You've completed your first exploration of an assurance case. You've learned that:

  • Assurance cases break down big claims into smaller, provable parts
  • They create a clear chain of reasoning from goals to evidence
  • They make assumptions and boundaries explicit through context
  • They can help build trust in complex systems like AI

What's Next?‚Äã

In Module 2, you'll move from exploring to creating. You'll learn to build your own arguments, starting with simple goals and strategies. The Fair Recruitment AI case you explored today will serve as your reference model.

Remember The power of TEA isn't just in the structure ‚Äì it's in making complex arguments transparent and challengeable. By breaking down "trust" into specific, evidence-backed claims, we can have meaningful conversations about whether systems are truly trustworthy. :::

This module used an "Experience Before Explanation" approach. You explored first, then learned the terminology. This is how experts actually learn ‚Äì by doing, not by memorizing definitions. Keep this experiential mindset as you continue your TEA journey!

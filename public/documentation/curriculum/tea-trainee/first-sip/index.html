Module Introduction

Imagine you're about to use a new AI system that screens job applications at your company. The vendor claims it's "fair and unbiased".

How can you be sure that the vendor's claim is true? What reasoning has been provided to assure you that it won't discriminate against certain groups? What evidence would convince you?

In this module, you'll explore an approach to answering these questions known as Trustworthy and Ethical Assurance (or TEA for short). But rather than starting with definitions and theory, let's dive straight into a real example.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

Identify the key components of an assurance case

Recognize goals, strategies, property claims, evidence, and context elements in structured arguments

Explain how structured arguments build trustworthiness

Understand the logical flow from high-level claims down to concrete evidence and how decomposition creates clarity

Trace argument chains in real assurance cases

Follow the decomposition from goals through strategies to supporting evidence and understand their relationships

Evaluate the strength and completeness of assurance arguments

Critically assess what makes arguments convincing and identify where they may be weak or incomplete

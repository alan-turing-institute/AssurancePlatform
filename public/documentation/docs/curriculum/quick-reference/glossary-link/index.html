Most Common Termsâ€‹

Here are the most frequently used terms you'll encounter when working with the TEA Platform:

Core Conceptsâ€‹

  • Assurance - Process of building justified trust through structured argumentation
  • Assurance Case - Structured argument demonstrating a system meets its goals
  • Argument - Logical structure linking claims to evidence
  • Trustworthy and Ethical Assurance (TEA) - The methodology and platform for argument-based assurance

Element Typesâ€‹

  • Goal Claim - Top-level normative claim directing the assurance case
  • Property Claim - Specific claim about a system property
  • Strategy - Explanation of how goals/claims are decomposed
  • Evidence - Information supporting claims
  • Context - Scope, boundaries, and constraints

Evidence & Qualityâ€‹

  • Evidence Quality - Assessment of evidence using three criteria: relevance, reliability, sufficiency
  • Relevance - How directly evidence supports a claim
  • Reliability - How trustworthy the evidence source is
  • Sufficiency - Whether there's enough evidence to support the claim

Methodologyâ€‹

  • Deliberation - Careful consideration of different perspectives
  • Critical Reflection - Systematic examination of assumptions and reasoning
  • Justified Trust - Trust based on evidence and reasoning
  • Stakeholder - Anyone with interest in or affected by the system
